ðŸ”¬ Introduction to Molecular Biology

Molecular biology focuses on understanding the mechanisms by which genetic information encoded in DNA is used to produce the proteins that determine cell structure and function. This field bridges the gap between genetics and biochemistry.

Three core processes are central to molecular biology:

  • Replication: The process by which DNA makes a copy of itself during cell division.
  • Transcription: The conversion of DNA information into messenger RNA (mRNA).
  • Translation: The process by which mRNA is decoded to build proteins, the functional molecules in cells.

ðŸ“– How It Works

In translation, the sequence of codons in mRNA is interpreted by the ribosome to assemble a chain of amino acids, forming a protein. Each group of three RNA nucleotides forms a codon that corresponds to one amino acid or a stop signal.

  • Start Codon: AUG â€” signals the beginning of translation and codes for Methionine.
  • Stop Codons: UAA, UAG, UGA â€” signal the end of translation.

The ribosome moves along the RNA sequence three nucleotides at a time, matching each codon to its corresponding amino acid using transfer RNA (tRNA). The amino acids are linked together to form a polypeptide chain, which folds into a functional protein.
