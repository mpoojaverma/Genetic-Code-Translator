üî¨ Introduction to Molecular Biology

Molecular biology is a branch of science that explores the structure and function of genes at a molecular level. It is primarily concerned with understanding the interactions between DNA, RNA, and proteins within cells.

  • Replication: DNA copies itself for cell division.
  • Transcription: DNA is transcribed into mRNA.
  • Translation: mRNA is translated into proteins.

These processes together are referred to as the Central Dogma of Molecular Biology: DNA ‚Üí RNA ‚Üí Protein.

üìñ How Translation Works

Translation is the final step in the expression of genes. It converts genetic code from RNA into a sequence of amino acids to form proteins. Codons, or 3-letter nucleotide sequences, are read by ribosomes and matched to corresponding amino acids.

  • Start Codon (AUG): Initiates translation and codes for Methionine.
  • Stop Codons (UAA, UAG, UGA): End the translation process.

Each codon is matched with an amino acid by tRNA molecules, creating a growing peptide chain.
